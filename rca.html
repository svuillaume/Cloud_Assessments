Identity Risk Commentary

80% of breaches in the public cloud are caused by stolen or compromised identities — making identity the #1 attack vector in cloud environments (CrowdStrike Global Threat Report). FortiCNAPP's CIEM analysis reveals a pattern of persistent over-privilege across cloud identities. Admin accounts with full administrative rights and no MFA represent the single highest-risk condition in the environment — a single successful phishing attack on any such account would grant an adversary unrestricted access to cloud resources, billing controls, and data stores.

Standing privilege — permanent admin access that is always active rather than granted on demand — violates the principle of least privilege and is a recognized compliance failure under CIS, NIST, and most regulatory frameworks. The right-sizing of these entitlements should be treated as a compliance requirement, not a best-effort improvement.

Dormant and unused entitlements represent an invisible attack surface. Identities that have not used the majority of their granted permissions in the past 90 days should be reduced to their observed access pattern. FortiCNAPP can generate right-sized permission recommendations to accelerate this remediation.

▲ Risk Summary — Why Immediate Action Matters

59 admin identity(ies) with full cloud privileges have MFA disabled. A single phishing email targeting any one of these accounts grants an attacker unrestricted access to every cloud resource — compute, storage, networking, and billing controls. Admin accounts without MFA are the leading vector for cloud account takeover. Enforcing MFA on these accounts alone reduces account compromise risk by 99.9% against automated and credential-based attacks. This is the single highest-priority remediation in this report.
